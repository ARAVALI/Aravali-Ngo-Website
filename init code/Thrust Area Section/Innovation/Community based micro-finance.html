Community based micro-finance

ARAVALI has evolved its own model of community based microfinance. The core of this model are the following six elements.

1. Financialassetbuilding
2. Institutionbuilding
3. Human development
4. Physicalandnaturalassetbuilding
5. Facilitatingentitlementsandschematicsupport
6. Establishingthecollaborativepolygon

This model has been tested and applied by our partner organisations for their SHG Programming.

ARAVALI has helped over 40 organisations understand their roles better in the promotion and nurturing of self-help groups and leading them to take on more evolved roles in livelihoods generation. The focus is laid on human resource development and creation of appropriate systems to facilitate effective credit flows and building livelihoods security. An important area has been risk reduction through micro-insurance. ARAVALI has supported in building capacities of human resource in the partner organisations and also provided professional assistance through placement of professionals for developing community base microfinance programme of the organisation.

This initiative of ARAVALI have helped reach out to around 10,000 families. Over time, ARAVALI supports Partners to evolve their initiative into a Family Livelihoods Resource Centre to enable them to work with the families that are associated with SHGs.
