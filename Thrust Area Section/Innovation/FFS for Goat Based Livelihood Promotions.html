Thrust Areas > Innovations > FFS for Goat Based Livelihood Promotions

In its effort to reach out to poor and marginal ARAVALI has been continuously working on the livelihood issues with the partner NGOs in evolving appropriate approaches for enhancement of livelihoods of the poor. ARAVALI initiated working on Farmer Field School (FFS) participatory extension methodology in 2007 with two partner organizations in Ajmer District on Kharif and Rabi crop. It was realized that working on agriculture is restricted by the erratic nature of the rainfall. It has also been our learning working on livelihoods in Rajasthan, that marginalized communities in particular and rural households in general are engaged in goat rearing across the state and this contributes significantly to their subsistence. Therefore, in principle, it was agreed to innovate and pilot Farmer Field School as an extension methodology for promoting goat based livelihoods.

ARAVALI took support of the Agriculture Man Ecology Foundation (AMEF), Bangalore that had Master trainers of FFS, certified by FAO (Rome) and had pioneered this methodology in Agriculture across the country. However, in goat rearing, use of FFS as a pedagogy had never been piloted. With AMEF’s support, ARAVALI and her partner organizations from different Districts of Rajasthan, took up the learning and action challenge to first understand the methodology and then introduce it in small ruminant-based livelihood promotion initiatives of partner organizations with a purpose to develop a trained cadre of Facilitators in organisations to work on Goat based livelihood programme.

In January 2008, an orientation workshop on the FFS methodology was organized by ARAVALI for building understanding of the ARAVALI and its select partner organisations. This was followed by a Short Term Training of Facilitators (SToF) which was tied up with a season-long practice by the SToF-trained Facilitator-trainees with groups of goat rearers in their respective regions. A subsequent evaluation of the Facilitators by AMEF and ARAVALI in late 2009 resulted in the certification of 11 trainees from 10 partner organizations of ARAVALI as Certified FFS Facilitators.In the first quarter of 2010, a small assessment was done by ARAVALI in this context to understand the present engagement of trained Facilitators and the organisations’ plan to work on the goat based livelihoods using the FFS methodology. From the assessment, it came out quite clearly that all the organizations fathom the need for working on the goat rearing as a livelihood activity of the poor and realize the efficacy of FFS as methodology to catalyse learning and application for enhanced benefits to poor families from goat rearing.

ARAVALI has prepared a detailed manual in English on the Farmer Field School extension methodology for goat based livelihoods.
